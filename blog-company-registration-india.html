Step-by-Step Guide to Company Registration in India

LAUNCHING YOUR BUSINESS LEGALLY AND STRATEGICALLY

December 20, 2024 Company Law I-NEXUS Legal Team

Transforming a business idea into a legal entity is a landmark achievement for any entrepreneur. In India, the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA) has streamlined this process, but precision remains vital for a successful incorporation.

The Road to Incorporation: A 5-Step Guide

Step 1: Digital Signature Certificate (DSC)

Since the registration process is entirely online, the first requirement is obtaining a Digital Signature Certificate for the proposed directors. This serves as an electronic tool for signing statutory documents.

Step 2: Director Identification Number (DIN)

Every individual intended to be a director must apply for a DIN. This is a unique identification number that stays with the director for life, regardless of how many companies they join.

Step 3: Name Approval via RUN

The company name must be unique and not resemble any existing company or trademark. You can use the "Reserve Unique Name" (RUN) service on the MCA portal to propose and secure your desired name.

Step 4: Filing the SPICe+ Form

The SPICe+ (Simplified Proforma for Incorporating Company Electronically Plus) is an integrated web form that covers several requirements in one go:

  • Incorporation Application
  • DIN Allotment
  • PAN and TAN Applications
  • EPFO and ESIC Registrations
  • Opening of Bank Account

Step 5: Certificate of Incorporation

Once the Registrar of Companies (RoC) reviews and approves the documents, a Certificate of Incorporation (CoI) is issued. This certificate includes the Corporate Identity Number (CIN), officially bringing your company to life.

Essential Documents Required

  • Identity Proof: PAN Card of directors (Mandatory).
  • Address Proof: Voter ID, Passport, or Driving License.
  • Registered Office Proof: Latest utility bill and a No Objection Certificate (NOC) from the owner.
  • MOA & AOA: The Memorandum and Articles of Association defining the company's constitution and rules.
